About Me

Over the past semester, my internship at Colonial Life Arena has provided invaluable hands-on experience in the dynamic world of sport and entertainment management.

 

 

My name is Jeb Bell, and I am a senior at the University of South Carolina majoring in Sport and Entertainment Management.

For the past 15 weeks, I have been an intern at Colonial Life Arena in Columbia, South Carolina. This internship has been a cornerstone of my college experience, aligning perfectly with my Graduation with Leadership Distinction (GLD) pathway. My role at Colonial Life Arena has been multifaceted—ranging from setting up and dismantling basketball courts to building concert stages, managing logistics for large-scale events such as Disney on Ice and rodeos, and repairing broken infrastructure within the venue.

These experiences have not only allowed me to contribute meaningfully to real-world projects but have also expanded my understanding of team dynamics, operational planning, and adaptability in high-pressure environments. My e-portfolio is organized to reflect the integration of classroom learning and beyond-the-classroom experiences, highlighting transformative insights and practical applications that have shaped my personal and professional development.

Leadership

... the ability to inspire, guide, and empower others toward a shared goal while adapting to challenges and fostering collaboration.

A professional goal I aim to pursue is to lead operations for a large-scale entertainment or sports venue, ensuring that events and maintenance systems run smoothly and efficiently. During my internship at Colonial Life Arena, one of the most consistent challenges I observed was a lack of clear communication between departments, which often led to delays, confusion, and inefficiencies during event transitions. This issue affects multiple stakeholders, including operations teams, performers, vendors, and ultimately the audience, as even minor missteps can ripple into larger disruptions. To address this, I propose implementing a centralized project management system—such as Trello or Asana—that would allow all departments to communicate in real time, update task progress, and flag any issues or changes. This solution is grounded in my key insight about the importance of adaptability and teamwork, which I developed while coordinating ice rink construction and managing tight event turnarounds. The system would promote accountability, transparency, and flexibility, making it easier to adapt when schedules shift or complications arise.

To implement this solution, I would begin by collaborating with department heads to identify workflow needs and assign team members to manage the new system. Training sessions would be conducted to ensure all users are familiar with the platform’s features. The system would first be piloted during a smaller event, such as a local concert, to monitor its effectiveness on a manageable scale. Tasks would be categorized by department (e.g., staging, facilities, lighting), and deadlines and responsibilities would be clearly outlined. Each team would be responsible for updating their task progress and flagging any issues. Following the pilot event, the plan would scale up for larger, more complex productions.

To evaluate the success of the plan, I would gather data on task completion times, compare timelines to previous events, and distribute post-event surveys to staff to assess their experiences with the new system. Input would come from event managers, department supervisors, and frontline workers who engage with the platform directly. Key metrics for success would include a noticeable reduction in delays, higher satisfaction ratings from staff, and improved coordination across departments. From this experience, I hope to deepen my understanding of systems-based leadership, refine my problem-solving skills, and develop a model that I can bring into future operational roles in the entertainment or sports industry. This initiative not only addresses an immediate issue I encountered but also prepares me to lead with intention, clarity, and collaboration in high-stakes environments.
